✦ Smart Pricing Tips

Wisdom for Women Who Mean Business

💡

The True Cost of "Free Delivery"

When you offer free delivery, you pay for it. Fuel, time, and vehicle wear are real costs. Add them to your product price or charge separately — never absorb them silently.

⚡

Split Overhead by Units

Divide your monthly rent, electricity, and water by units produced. If you make 50 items and rent is ₦50,000/month, each item carries ₦1,000 of overhead — that's money you must recover.

🏦

Pay Yourself First

Your time is a cost. Set an hourly rate for yourself — even a modest one — and include it in every unit price. If the business can't afford to pay you, the price needs to go up, not your hours.

📦

Packaging Is Not Optional

Boxes, bags, labels, tissue paper, tape — they're all costs. Calculate packaging per unit, not in bulk. A beautiful package increases perceived value and can justify a higher price point.

🛡️

Build In a Buffer

Add a 5–10% contingency to every price. Prices rise, suppliers change, unexpected costs happen. A buffer means you never suddenly start making a loss because fuel went up this month.

📈

Review Prices Quarterly

Costs change every 3 months — raw material prices, fuel, utilities. A price set in January may be losing you money by April. Set a calendar reminder to recalculate every quarter.
